FAQs

Common questions

Everything members ask when they first join ZURI.

Any student currently enrolled at Zetech University is eligible to join ZURI — regardless of your department or year of study. We welcome students from all faculties: ICT, Business, Health Sciences, and more.
There is a small annual membership contribution used to fund club activities, events, and resources. The current amount is communicated during onboarding. Scholarship arrangements are available for members facing financial hardship — just reach out to the club leadership.
After joining ZURI, you'll be invited to a project orientation session where you select your focus area. Teams are then formed based on skills, interests, and project needs. You can also propose your own project idea and recruit teammates.
None specifically. ZURI is a learning environment — we welcome beginners and experienced builders alike. Whether your strength is coding, design, research, business development, or communication, there's a role for you on every project team.
General club meetings are held bi-weekly. Project teams typically meet once or twice per week depending on sprint cycles and deadlines. Events and workshops are scheduled monthly and communicated in advance via WhatsApp and email.
Absolutely. ZURI actively encourages member-driven ideas. Submit your project proposal using the template in the resources section, and present it at the next general meeting. The club will provide feedback, mentorship, and possibly team support.
Projects can access funding through the club's internal budget, university grants, and partnership programmes with IZET Hub and external sponsors. Teams that win hackathons and competitions also receive direct prize funding. We also help teams apply for external innovation grants.
